History

Baseball Hack Day was orginally born out of a Boston area in 2012 by members of news & geek meetup group Hacks/Hackers and Society of American Baseball Research Boston Chapter. Our first event was held at The Boston Globe on March 24, 2012 with 35 participants with 9 projects. In 2013, thoughtbot hosted a 31-participants, 10-projects fun and inspiring hack day in March. In 2014, Baseball Hack day expanded into two cities, Boston and Philadelphia. Since then, this annual, grass-roots, volunteer-run and open-sourced project have had events in Boston, Philadelphia, Montreal, Chicago, San Francisco, San Diego and Toronto! MLB cut 4 article, 2017.

The event is based on the principal of the Hack Day Manifesto. It will always be free to attend, and we are run by volunteers, and we’ll never be for-profit. It's a collective labor of love. If you want to join us in the fun, read this doc, then join our slack channel and reach out. Can't wait to hear from you!
